Privacy Policy

Your data, your rights — transparency you can trust.

📅 Last Updated: June 1, 2026

DataSciencePro (“we”, “our”, “us”, or “the Company”) is dedicated to safeguarding your privacy. This policy describes the types of personal information we collect, how we use and share it, and the choices you have regarding your data when you interact with our website, mobile applications, learning platforms, and related services (collectively, the “Services”).

1. Information We Collect

We collect information that you voluntarily provide, automatically through usage, and in some cases from trusted third parties.

📋 Personal Data

  • Identity & contact details — Full name, email address, phone number (optional).
  • Social login data — When you sign in via Google, Facebook, LinkedIn, or other OAuth providers, we receive your public profile, email address, and basic profile information (subject to your privacy settings on those platforms).
  • Professional & learning data — Course progress, quiz results, certificates, and any data you submit through forums or support requests.
  • User-generated content — Comments, project submissions, feedback, or survey responses.

📊 Automatically Collected Data

  • Usage & analytics — Pages visited, time spent, clicks, features used, and error logs.
  • Device & browser information — IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers, and approximate location (city/region level).
  • Cookies & similar trackers — We use essential, functional, and analytics cookies to enhance site performance and remember preferences.

2. How We Use Your Information

We rely on legitimate business interests, contractual necessity, and legal compliance to process your data. Specifically, we use your information for:

  • ✅ Providing, personalizing, and maintaining your account and our Services.
  • ✅ Authenticating users and preventing unauthorized access.
  • ✅ Improving user experience, platform performance, and content recommendations.
  • ✅ Responding to customer support tickets, technical issues, or requests.
  • ✅ Ensuring platform security, detecting fraud, and enforcing our terms.
  • ✅ Sending service-related announcements (e.g., policy updates, security alerts). We do not sell your email for marketing without consent.
  • ✅ Conducting internal research and data analysis to enhance our educational offerings.

3. Third-Party Services & Integrations

Our platform integrates with external partners to provide seamless functionality. These services have their own privacy practices, and we encourage you to review their policies:

  • Social login & sharing — Facebook, Google, LinkedIn, GitHub.
  • Analytics providers — Google Analytics, Mixpanel (aggregated, anonymized insights).
  • Messaging & support — WhatsApp Business API, Zendesk, or Intercom for customer support interactions.
  • Payment processing — If you purchase premium content, payments are handled via PCI-DSS compliant gateways (Stripe/Razorpay); we never store full credit card details.

Any information shared with these providers is limited to what is necessary to perform the service (e.g., email address for login, payment confirmation, message routing).

4. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for advertising or monetary gain. However, limited disclosure may occur in the following scenarios:

  • Service providers & vendors: Trusted companies that help us with hosting, cloud storage (AWS, Google Cloud), analytics, email delivery, and customer support – all bound by confidentiality agreements.
  • Legal compliance: If required by law, subpoena, or governmental request to protect our rights, users’ safety, or investigate fraud.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your data may be transferred with notice and continued protection under this policy.

5. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies to improve functionality, analyze traffic, and personalize content. You can manage cookie preferences via your browser settings. Disabling certain cookies may affect site features. Types of cookies we use:

  • Strictly necessary: Authentication, security, and load balancing.
  • Performance/Analytics: To count visits and traffic sources.
  • Functional: Remembering your language or region preferences.

For more details, see our Cookie Preference Center (available on site footer).

6. Data Security & Retention

We implement industry-standard technical, administrative, and physical safeguards to protect your personal data: encryption in transit (TLS 1.3), secure data centers, access controls, and regular vulnerability assessments. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. We retain your data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy or as required by law (e.g., tax, legal records). Inactive accounts may be anonymized after 24 months of inactivity.

7. Your Privacy Rights (GDPR, CCPA, LGPD)

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

🔍 Right to Access ✏️ Right to Rectification 🗑️ Right to Deletion (Right to be Forgotten) 📤 Data Portability ⛔ Right to Opt-Out of Sale (we don’t sell data) 🚫 Right to Restrict Processing 📢 Right to Withdraw Consent

To exercise your rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer using the details below. We will respond within 30 days (or as required by applicable law). You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

8. Children’s Privacy

DataSciencePro does not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13 (or under 16 in certain jurisdictions). If we become aware that a child has provided us with personal data without verifiable parental consent, we will delete that information promptly. Please contact us if you believe we may have such data.

9. International Data Transfers

We operate globally and may store or process your data in countries other than your own (including the United States, India, and EU regions). Where required by law, we implement Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or rely on adequacy decisions to ensure your data receives an equivalent level of protection.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may revise this policy from time to time to reflect legal, technical, or business updates. We will notify you of material changes via email or prominent notice on our website before the changes become effective. The “Last Updated” date at the top indicates when this policy was last revised. Your continued use of the Services after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
